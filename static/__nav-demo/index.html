Full nav proposal · 12 ideas

  1. Mobile swipe gesturesdemo ↗

    Touch swipe between top sections — your explicit ask. Order: Home → About → Gallery → Posts → Resume → Portfolio. Hash routing for deep links. ~150 LOC, works alongside the existing hamburger.

  2. Command palette (Cmd+K)demo ↗

    One keystroke fuzzy-search across pages, posts, galleries, tags, and quick actions (toggle theme, download resume, email). Powers the entire navigation experience for power users.

  3. Breadcrumbs on inner pagesstructural

    Right now a post just says “← All posts”. A real breadcrumb (Home › Posts › The Mostly Maine Trip) gives orientation without taking space. Same for galleries and tag pages.

  4. Sticky in-page TOC for long postsdelight

    The TOC partial already exists for posts with toc: true. On wide screens, pin it to the right margin so readers can jump between sections without losing place. Auto-highlights the active section.

  5. Reading progress hairlinedelight

    2-pixel sliver at top of viewport that fills with --accent as you scroll a post. ~10 LOC. Long posts (12-min website tutorial) feel less daunting.

  6. Section indicator in the sticky headermobile

    When you scroll past an h2 on a long post, the header shows the current section name in addition to the post title. Notion does this. Tiny IntersectionObserver implementation.

  7. Adjacent-section nav at the bottom of every page

    Posts already have prev/next. Apply the pattern to top-level pages too — /about/ bottom-links to /gallery/, etc. Reinforces the swipe order on devices without touch.

  8. Back-to-top pill

    After scrolling >2 viewports, fade in a small floating button anchored bottom-right that scrolls smoothly to top. Auto-hides on scroll-up. Standard, low-cost, surprisingly missed.

  9. Tag chips at the top of post archive

    Right now /posts/ is a flat list. Add a row of clickable tag chips above (RC, photography, website, …) for one-click filtering. The data is already in tagMap.

  10. Gallery year/season filter

    /gallery/ shows 13 albums. Adding tiny filter chips (2024 · 2023 · 2022 · …) on top makes browsing a portfolio of years much faster.

  11. Keyboard shortcut overlay (press ?)power-user

    A simple help dialog that lists the keys: g h home, g p posts, g g gallery, g r resume, cmd k palette, t toggle theme. Builds the muscle memory.

  12. RSS + GitHub source links in footer

    You emit /index.xml but the footer doesn’t link to it. Add a small RSS icon plus “View source” to the repo. Free credibility for a developer site.

What I'd ship first

If I had to pick a single batch: the mobile swipe nav (your ask), breadcrumbs, and the back-to-top pill. Together they cover ~80% of the orientation problems on the current site, are mostly additive, and don't change the editorial-calm look. Cmd+K is a power move I'd ship a week later.
